# North Stars

1. I want to continue to write songs.
1. I want to continue being asked to perform sometimes. A few times per year.
1. I don't want to spend money, or involve other people's money.
1. I want the songs to be collected in a simple, coherent way, for myself mostly, but also for a handful of people that would care. 
1. I don't want an artistic/public persona that I have to manage. I realize that this is in conflict with the above, but my solution is to do things as passively as possible. I don't really like for people that I have to work and interact with to know about my songs, so I like to keep that part of my life somewhat hidden.
1. I would like there to exist at least one recording of each song. I think it could be nice for there to be multiple recordings, with none definitive.
1. If I make any money, I would like to pay off Douglas since he lost money printing my record. 

# Projects

1. I would like to maintain spenking.spencerkingman.com, perhaps adding recordings.
1. I would like to create a binder that collects the material from the website in hard copy.
1. I would like to continue to add videos and other content to various sites youtube.com, soundcloud.com, etc.
1. As the Crosses album is already on Spotify, I would like to have some other, newer work on Spotify. I get embarrased when people find out I am musician and look on Spotify, only to find my most immature songs.


I imagine I would like to have a musical life like Connie Converse did, but without the posthumous revival. Just a private person who had a collection of works that she had ambivalent feelings about, some modest performances, a few recordings. She was able to gather them together, and organize them in a filing cabinet and leave them with her brother for "anyone who ever asks." 

I am also reminded of the old green filing cabinet in John Le Carre's "A Perfect Spy." It is a totem of his complicated relationship with his father. An object of fascination and revulsion with indeterminate value. I may have a hard time making albums and doing anything with old music because there are lots of unpleasant emotions associated with them. In a sense, the songs are like containers for hazardous feelings, and I don't particularly want to get into them.
